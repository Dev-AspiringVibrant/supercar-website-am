HISTORY

Creating your future

Trion Supercars, a company known for its high-performance hypercars, has its roots in the automotive landscape of 2013. Founded by Richard Patterson, a visionary entrepreneur with a passion for pushing the boundaries of automotive engineering, Trion Supercars emerged with the ambitious goal of creating a new breed of hypercar that would redefine luxury and performance. Their first creation, the Nemesis, was unveiled in 2016, showcasing an innovative design and a powerful engine that promised to deliver exceptional speed and handling. Since then, Trion has continued to refine its technology and expand its offerings, culminating in the development of the Nemesis RR, a fully electric hypercar that pushes the limits of performance and sustainability. Today, Trion stands as a testament to the relentless pursuit of innovation and excellence in the world of high-performance automobiles.

What Sets
Us Apart

What sets Trion Supercars apart is its cutting-edge approach to blending extreme performance with advanced technology and luxury. Trion pushes the limits of innovation by offering electric and hybrid powertrains that deliver impressive speed and acceleration, rivaling traditional supercars. The brand also leads the way in automotive design, with futuristic features like a fully digital cockpit and wireless charging.

In addition to performance, Trion is committed to sustainability, using eco-friendly materials without compromising on quality. The combination of power, high-tech connectivity, and a personalized driving experience sets Trion apart, positioning it as a standout in the competitive supercar market.

Richard
Patterson

Richard (Rich) Patterson, CEO & CTO of Trion Supercars, fulfilled his lifelong dream in May 2012 after 40 years in the automotive industry by founding Trion with a core team of experts.

Rich's passion for cars started in his Detroit childhood, and at 15, he earned a GM apprenticeship after winning a statewide drafting contest. A graduate of Mumford High School and a Hall of Fame inductee, Rich began his career as a CAD designer at Modern Engineering, quickly rising to leadership roles due to his expertise in automotive interior systems.

With over two decades of experience, including contributions to Chrysler's Stow 'n Go seating system, Rich moved to the West Coast to immerse himself in design innovation. Today, he brings his vision to life with Trion Supercars, offering high-performance American-made supercars that combine style, speeds of 300 mph, and comfort. Trion offers a variety of powertrain options, including traditional gasoline engines, hybrid systems, and fully electric models.
